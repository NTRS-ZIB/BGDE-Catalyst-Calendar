Structural Note — How to Read This Calendar

BGDE's forward catalysts are not fully independent. One event gates several others: the AI/HPC anchor tenant determines whether the Texas site produces revenue, whether project-level financing is available on reasonable terms, and whether the strategic/M&A case is credible. Separately, a second chain runs through the balance sheet — weak quarterly results pressure stockholders' equity, which pressures the Nasdaq $5M/quarter condition, which increases reliance on dilutive financing, which pressures the share price and therefore the Series D conversion ratio.

Texas Site ✅ Owned→ AI/HPC Anchor Tenant #3→ Project Financing→ Buildout >111 MW→ First AI/HPC Revenue #11
Quarterly Results #1, #6→ Stockholders' Equity→ Nasdaq $5M Test #7→ Financing Need→ Series D Dilution #10

Confidence levels below refer to timing certainty — will this happen when stated — not outcome. HIGH = statutory, contractual, or already-scheduled. MED = expected but unconfirmed. LOW = speculative. Because these catalysts are correlated rather than independent, treating them as separate probability draws overstates the number of ways this works out. Events already settled as of this report are grouped in Section 4 rather than mixed into the horizons below.

Section 8 · Methodology, Sources & Document Log

Confidence Scale

HIGH = statutory, contractual, or already-scheduled (quarterly filing deadlines; the Nov 14 annual-meeting commitment; the Aug 30 Series D conversion date). MED = expected but unconfirmed, or guided without a firm date. LOW = speculative, condition-gated, with no disclosed timeline. Confidence refers to timing certainty, not outcome — a HIGH-confidence catalyst can still resolve badly.

Fact vs. Assumption

Timing, description, and source lines are sourced statements traceable to filings or press releases. Purple-bordered blocks are analyst interpretation and are labeled as such. Impact and upside/downside framing is judgment, not disclosure.

Known Limitations

The Q2 2026 earnings date is a third-party estimate, not company-confirmed. BGDE has not published a fully-diluted share count reflecting Series D conversion plus warrants, so any dilution figure here is inference. The "up to 300 MW" Texas ceiling is a company projection contingent on ERCOT validation, gas buildout, permitting, and financing — none of which is secured. Per-MW revenue and margin figures originate from management's investor deck and are illustrative, not contracted. Sell-side coverage is effectively absent, so there is no consensus estimate to check management framing against.
